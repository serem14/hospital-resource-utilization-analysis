# Hospital Resource Utilization Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes hospital patient data to identify patterns in healthcare resource utilization. It demonstrates how data analytics can support hospitals in optimizing resource allocation, reducing unnecessary costs, and improving patient outcomes.

---

## Objectives
- Identify high-cost patients driving hospital expenditure  
- Analyze visit patterns across patient groups  
- Segment patients based on utilization and burden  
- Provide actionable insights for healthcare decision-making  

---

## Tools Used
- **SQL** – Data querying and analysis  
- **Excel** – Data preparation and feature engineering  
- **Power BI** – Data visualization and dashboard creation  

---

## SQL Analysis

The following analyses were performed:

- Identification of patients with frequent hospital visits  
- Cost distribution across age groups  
- High-burden patient segmentation  
- Patients with above-average total cost using subqueries  
- Comparison of visit frequency and cost patterns

## Key Insights

- Senior patients recorded the highest number of hospital visits, indicating increased demand for chronic disease management  
- A small proportion of patients contributed disproportionately to total hospital costs  
- High-burden patients represent a critical group requiring targeted intervention  
- Frequent low-cost visits suggest gaps in preventive care and outpatient management  

---

## Business & Clinical Impact

This analysis enables:

- Identification of high-cost and high-risk patients  
- Improved allocation of healthcare resources  
- Reduction of unnecessary hospital visits through preventive strategies  
- Data-driven decision-making in patient management  

---

## Recommendations

- Implement targeted follow-up programs for high-burden patients  
- Introduce chronic disease management programs for senior patients  
- Strengthen preventive care and patient education  
- Allocate resources based on patient segmentation rather than uniform distribution  

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates the role of data analytics in improving healthcare efficiency by identifying key patient segments, optimizing resource utilization, and supporting informed clinical and operational decisions.

This analysis demonstrates how data analytics can support efficient healthcare management through patient segmentation and cost analysis.
